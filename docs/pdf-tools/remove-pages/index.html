How to Remove Pages from a PDF

Step 1

Upload Your PDF

Drop or choose any PDF file. It loads locally in your browser — nothing is sent to any server.

Step 2

Click Pages to Delete

All pages start as green "Keep." Click any page to turn it red and mark it for removal. Click again to unmark.

Step 3

Use Range Input

Optionally type a range like 1, 3, 5-7 to quickly mark multiple pages for deletion at once.

Step 4

Download Clean PDF

Click "Remove Selected Pages & Download" and get a new PDF instantly with only the pages you kept.

Why Remove Pages from a PDF This Way?

Most PDF editors require a subscription, installation, or upload your files to a remote server. This tool removes pages directly in your browser using PDF.js and pdf-lib — your document never leaves your device. It's ideal for removing cover pages, blank pages, headers, footers, or any confidential pages before sharing a document.

  • No file size limits imposed by server upload quotas
  • Works with password-free PDFs of any length
  • Visual thumbnail preview so you know exactly what you're deleting
  • Range syntax (1, 3, 5-7) makes bulk removal fast
  • 100% free, no account or signup required

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I remove pages from a PDF? ▼
Upload your PDF, then click any page thumbnail to mark it for removal (it turns red with an ✕ badge). Once you've selected all pages you want deleted, click "Remove Selected Pages & Download" to get a new PDF with only the pages you kept.
Can I remove multiple pages at once? ▼
Yes. You can click individual thumbnails to mark them for removal, or use the range input (e.g. 1, 3, 5-7) to mark multiple pages at once. You can also click "Select All for Removal" and then deselect the pages you want to keep.
Is my PDF kept private? ▼
Absolutely. This tool runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript — your PDF file is never uploaded to any server. All processing happens locally on your device, so your document stays completely private.
